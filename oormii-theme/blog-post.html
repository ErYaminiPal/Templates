The Art of Layering Necklaces: a complete style guide

A

By Aanya Khanna

Founder & Lead Designer · Oormii Jewels

𝕏 P ⎘

The most common question we get isn't "which necklace should I buy?". It's: "how do I wear them together?". Here is the short, honest, ten-minute answer.

Layering necklaces is less about rules and more about tension: between lengths, textures, finishes, and the negative space your décolletage creates. Done well, it looks unstudied. Done badly, it looks like you're wearing a fishing net. Here's how to land squarely on the right side of that line.

1. Start with three lengths, not three necklaces

Forget about the pieces for a moment. Think about the heights at which they'll sit. A choker (14"), a mid-pendant (16–18"), and a longer drop (20–24"). That's your scaffold. Whatever you hang on it should respect those gaps.

2. Mix textures, match finishes

Your eye loves contrast in form, but gets tired by contrast in tone. So: a delicate cable chain with a beaded rope and a flat curb chain — yes. But keep them all in silver, or all in gold. Mixing finishes is possible but harder; save it for when you're confident.

If you only remember one thing: contrast the chain types, not the metals. Texture variety reads as intention. Metal variety reads as accident.

3. The pendant is the punctuation

Use one pendant — never two — and let it sit on whichever length feels most central. The Tear Drop on a 16" chain is our perennial favourite for the middle. The other layers stay quiet around it.

4. Negative space is jewellery too

Resist the urge to fill every inch. Leave gaps. Let your skin show. The expensive look isn't more — it's fewer, better, with breathing room between.

5. Test it in motion

Layered necklaces should look as good when you're walking, laughing, leaning forward as they do standing still. Move your shoulders. Tilt your chin. If anything tangles, or any piece disappears entirely, you've layered too tight or too tonally close.

A starter combination we love

  • 14" Sparkling Silver Choker — closest to the throat, the textural anchor
  • 16" Tear Drop Pendant — the centrepiece, the punctuation
  • 20" Infinity Chain — the drop, the breathing room, the quiet finish

Try it. Then break the rules and tell us what you discovered. We're @oormii_jewels — we always reply.
